# IB-Alpha Research Skill v2.0

## Purpose
Use this skill to produce an institutional-grade equity research memo for listed companies, with a bias toward A-share technology, infrastructure, and manufacturing names.

## Core principle
Always separate:
- **good company**
- **good business**
- **good price**
- **good timing**

Never confuse reported profit with recurring operating profit.  
Always distinguish:
- statutory earnings
- adjusted earnings
- cash earnings
- asset monetization gains
- valuation multiple expansion

## Workflow

### Module 1: Industry structure
Assess:
- market size and growth
- policy support / regulatory constraints
- industry chain position
- barriers to entry
- bargaining power
- substitution risk
- cycle stage

Output:
- industry attractiveness score (1-5)
- industry phase: emerging / scaling / mature / declining

### Module 2: Business model
Map:
- revenue streams
- pricing model
- delivery model
- customer profile
- expansion engine
- capex intensity
- working-capital profile

Output:
- business model summary
- key revenue driver table
- operating leverage map

### Module 3: Competitive moat
Assess moat by:
- resource access
- technology
- switching cost
- ecosystem position
- capital access
- execution / delivery capability

Output:
- moat matrix
- moat durability rating

### Module 4: Financial quality
Analyze:
- revenue quality
- gross margin / EBITDA / EBIT / net margin
- recurring vs non-recurring profit
- cash conversion
- capex intensity
- ROE / ROIC
- leverage and liquidity
- customer concentration

Output:
- financial red flags
- operating quality score

### Module 5: Valuation
Use at least 3 methods where relevant:
- PE / EV-EBITDA
- DCF
- SOTP
Optional:
- PB / replacement cost / NAV
- PEG
- dividend yield / FFO / AFFO for asset-heavy models

Rules:
- Use adjusted earnings when one-off gains are material
- For capex-heavy businesses, avoid relying only on PE
- For platform + asset models, run SOTP

Output:
- bear / base / bull valuation range
- key assumptions table
- mispricing diagnosis

### Module 6: Catalysts
Assess:
- earnings catalysts
- asset monetization
- policy catalysts
- order / capacity / utilization inflection
- margin improvement
- financing or balance-sheet relief

Output:
- 3-5 highest probability catalysts
- estimated timing window

### Module 7: Risk map
Assess:
- macro risk
- industry risk
- technology substitution
- execution risk
- customer concentration
- policy / compliance
- financing / dilution
- valuation de-rating

Output:
- risk matrix with probability and impact

### Module 8: Capital markets lens
Assess:
- market narrative
- what is already priced in
- ownership structure
- possible re-rating / de-rating drivers
- sentiment sensitivity

Output:
- “consensus vs variant view” section

### Module 9: Management & governance
Assess:
- promoter / controller quality
- capital allocation record
- incentives
- related-party transactions
- strategic consistency
- credibility of disclosures

Output:
- governance quality note

### Module 10: Investment conclusion
Answer:
1. Is this a structurally good industry?
2. Is this company a real winner?
3. Is the current price attractive?
4. What breaks the thesis?
5. What must be monitored quarterly?

Output:
- investment thesis in one paragraph
- rating: Avoid / Watch / Accumulate on weakness / Core holding candidate
- monitoring dashboard

## Required output structure
1. Executive summary
2. Industry & policy backdrop
3. Business model
4. Competitive position
5. Financial quality
6. Valuation
7. Catalysts
8. Risks
9. Management / governance
10. Final view
11. What would change my mind

## Analyst discipline rules
- Prefer primary sources: annual reports, interim reports, exchange filings, policy documents, official company material
- Identify one-off items explicitly
- Avoid thesis statements unsupported by evidence
- Separate fact, inference, and judgment
- For hot themes (AI, semiconductor, defense, biotech), highlight where expectations may exceed current monetization
- Provide a clean checklist for follow-up diligence

## Special add-on for computing power / AI infrastructure
When analyzing “算力” names, always split the chain into:
- chips
- servers
- data center / IDC / AIDC
- optical modules
- network interconnect
- liquid cooling / thermal management
- power / UPS
- cloud / scheduling platform
- REIT / asset monetization angle

Then identify:
- which layer captures scarcity
- which layer has the best margin structure
- which layer is currently most crowded in valuation
